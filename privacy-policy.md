# Privacy Policy — bookmarX

**Last updated:** April 2026

## Summary

bookmarX stores everything locally on your device. No data is collected, transmitted, or shared with anyone — not with us, not with third parties.

## What the extension accesses

bookmarX intercepts the browser's internal network requests on x.com and twitter.com to capture bookmark data as it loads. This is the only way to access your bookmarks since X does not provide a public API for third-party apps.

## What data is stored

The following data is saved to Chrome's local extension storage (`chrome.storage.local`) on your device only:

- Tweet text, author name, and handle
- Tweet URL and date bookmarked
- Media type (photo, video, thread, post)
- Hashtags
- Your collections, keywords, and tags

## What data leaves your device

None. bookmarX has no backend, no server, and no analytics. Your bookmarks never leave your browser.

## Third-party services

bookmarX does not use any third-party services, trackers, or analytics tools.

## Data retention

All data is stored locally and persists until you uninstall the extension or clear its storage manually. You can export or delete your data at any time from within the extension.
